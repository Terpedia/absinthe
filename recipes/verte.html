Verte

Green / classic

The reference frame: alpine, bitter, bright, and louche-friendly. This concept preserves the classic trinity of wormwood, anise, and fennel, then uses late green botanicals to widen the aromatic halo.

Sensory target

What it should feel like

Start with high herbal lift, move into cool anise sweetness, and finish with dry wormwood bitterness. The palate should read green rather than sugary, with a louche that looks opaline jade.

Herbs

Botanical frame

  • Grand wormwood: bitter backbone and absinthe signature.
  • Green anise: sweet aromatic spine and louche support.
  • Florence fennel: rounder sweetness with herbal depth.
  • Hyssop: resinous green top notes and classic color contribution.
  • Lemon balm: fresh citron lift without breaking style.
  • Pontica: softer wormwood tones for complexity.

Molecules

Primary aromatic drivers

  • Anethole: the core anise-fennel sweetness and louche behavior.
  • Fenchone: dry, slightly camphoraceous contrast to anethole.
  • Thujone: wormwood-associated bitter-aromatic marker.
  • Linalool: floral lift that softens the bitter frame.
  • Citral: lemon balm brightness in the top note.
  • Chlorophylls: green visual identity in naturally colored variants.

Color logic

Why it reads green

The visual identity comes from post-base green botanicals rather than artificial coloring: hyssop, melissa, and pontica all reinforce the classic verte signal.
