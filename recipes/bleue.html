Bleue

Clear / transparent

A clear absinthe concept that skips the green coloring step and instead leans into precision, brightness, and a clean louche on dilution.

Sensory target

What it should feel like

Crystal-clear in the bottle and silvery-opaline when diluted. The aroma should be focused, brisk, and elegant, with fewer leafy tones than a verte and a slightly more architectural bitterness.

Herbs

Botanical frame

  • Grand wormwood: clean bitter signature.
  • Green anise: louche and sweet aromatic center.
  • Florence fennel: support and softness.
  • Coriander seed: dry citrus-spice lift.
  • Angelica root: earthy fixation and structure.
  • Pontica: optional nuance without visible green extraction.

Molecules

Primary aromatic drivers

  • Anethole: essential for louche and sweet spice shape.
  • Fenchone: dries out the finish.
  • Alpha-pinene: crisp terpene lift from supporting botanicals.
  • Limonene: bright citrus edge from coriander.
  • Thujone: wormwood-associated bitter note.
  • Terpinen-4-ol: subtle aromatic bridge through the mid-palate.

Color logic

Why it stays clear

The identity is defined by restraint: no green coloration phase, no red infusion, just a transparent spirit that blooms when water reveals the louche.
