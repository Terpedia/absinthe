Detour

Blue to purple / no licorice

A deliberate departure from the usual anise-led licorice profile. Lemon balm carries the freshness, butterfly pea handles the chromatic drama, and the overall shape reads herbal-citrus rather than confectionary.

Sensory target

What it should feel like

More like a mountain cordial crossed with an absinthe idea: cool herbs, citrus leaf, soft bitterness, and a color shift from blue toward purple when acidity enters the glass.

Herbs

Botanical frame

  • Lemon balm: primary aromatic signature and green-citrus lift.
  • Butterfly pea flower: blue chromatic source and theatrical shift.
  • Roman wormwood or gentler bitter herbs: keeps an absinthe echo without heavy licorice.
  • Hyssop: cool herbal brightness.
  • Verbena or lemon verbena: sharpened citrus line.
  • Angelica root: grounding structure.

Molecules

Primary aromatic drivers

  • Citral: lemon-balm brightness and the main top-note cue.
  • Rosmarinic acid: herbal depth often associated with lemon balm.
  • Ternatins: butterfly pea pigments responsible for blue-to-purple shift.
  • Linalool: floral-citrus smoothing across the profile.
  • Verbascoside-related phenolics: supportive herbal complexity from verbena-style botanicals.
  • Bitters from wormwood-family herbs: enough spine to keep it from reading like tea.

Color logic

Why it shifts blue to purple

Butterfly pea contributes blue anthocyanin-like pigments that visually swing purple in a more acidic environment. Lemon elements support the narrative even when the acid source sits outside the core herb bill.
