Rouge

Red / hibiscus

A red-leaning absinthe concept that keeps wormwood and anise intact while adding hibiscus for tart floral color and a brighter, more wine-like arc.

Sensory target

What it should feel like

The opening should suggest cranberry, pomegranate, and rose without turning into a liqueur. Bitterness stays present, but the mid-palate picks up hibiscus snap and a vivid ruby impression.

Herbs

Botanical frame

  • Grand wormwood: essential bitter axis.
  • Green anise: keeps it recognizably absinthe.
  • Florence fennel: rounds the sharp hibiscus edge.
  • Hibiscus calyx: tartness, color, and red-fruit illusion.
  • Rosehip or rose petal: optional support for a fuller floral bridge.
  • Lemon balm: keeps the top note lively and not jammy.

Molecules

Primary aromatic drivers

  • Anethole: the recognizable absinthe sweet-spice core.
  • Fenchone: dry structure against floral tartness.
  • Anthocyanins: red hue from hibiscus and pH-sensitive vibrancy.
  • Citric and malic acids: brightness that sharpens the profile.
  • Geraniol: rosy lift when petals or rosehip are present.
  • Linalool: floral smoothing across the transition from anise to hibiscus.

Color logic

Why it reads red

Hibiscus does the heavy lifting, delivering a saturated crimson tone. The red identity works best when the visual aim is jewel-like rather than opaque.
